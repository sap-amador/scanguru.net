Get Involved

Help bring AI medical imaging to the clinics that need it most. ScanGuru Ambassadors are volunteers who support our Heal for All mission through outreach, education, and community — in their own regions and languages.

A volunteer community program — not a job. Ambassador roles are voluntary and unpaid, focused entirely on outreach, education, and community. They are not employment, and they never involve clinical work, interpreting scans, or representing medical or performance claims.

Why it matters

Through our Heal for All program, ScanGuru provides free access to verified low-income-country clinics, children's cancer hospitals, and charity hospitals. Reaching those communities — in the right places, and in the right languages — is something we can't do alone.

How you can help

Pick the way that fits you. Every role is non-clinical and community-focused.

Community outreach

Connect us with clinics, NGOs, and hospitals in underserved regions that could qualify for free Heal for All access.

Education & awareness

Support awareness sessions and share how responsible AI imaging works — with students, community groups, and local healthcare networks.

Language & localization

Help us reach communities in their own language by reviewing our public and educational materials — marketing and community content, never clinical reports.

Academic & research network

Introduce us to universities and researchers interested in our research-use pilots.

What the role is not

To keep patients safe and our claims honest, ambassadors never:

Interpret or validate scans

Reading images and confirming findings is the work of qualified clinicians — not volunteers.

Make medical or performance claims

Ambassadors don't speak to accuracy, diagnosis, or regulatory status on ScanGuru's behalf.

Handle patient data

No access to patient images, reports, or any protected health information.

Who it's for

Students, healthcare advocates, community organizers, multilingual volunteers — anyone who wants to help widen access to medical imaging. No technical background required.
